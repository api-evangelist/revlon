# Revlon (revlon)

Revlon is a global beauty company whose portfolio spans color cosmetics, fragrance, skin care, hair color, hair care, beauty tools, and antiperspirants / deodorants. The company markets Revlon, Elizabeth Arden, Almay, Mitchum, Cutex, Charlie, SinfulColors, and Ultima II, along with a portfolio of licensed prestige and celebrity fragrance brands.

Revlon Consumer Products Corporation filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection in the Southern District of New York on **June 15, 2022** and emerged on **May 2, 2023** as a privately held company controlled by its former lenders (including BrandCo noteholders such as King Street Capital, Glendon Capital, and Angelo Gordon), having cut roughly **$2.7 billion** of debt from its balance sheet. Today Revlon operates as a private company with reduced leverage, selling through mass-market retail and prestige channels worldwide.

Revlon does not publish a public developer API. Supplier, retailer, and partner integrations occur through private commercial channels (e-commerce platform integrations on Shopify, EDI / GDSN feeds for retailers, and the corporate procurement portal).

## Corporate History

- Founded **1932** by Charles Revson, Joseph Revson, and Charles Lachman.
- Long-time controlling owner: MacAndrews & Forbes (Ronald Perelman) from 1985 through the 2022 bankruptcy.
- **June 15, 2022** — Revlon Consumer Products Corporation files Chapter 11 in the SDNY (Case No. 22-10760).
- **May 2, 2023** — emerges from Chapter 11 as a private company; equity is wiped out and ownership transfers to former BrandCo / Term Loan lenders. Approximately **$2.7B** of debt eliminated.
- Now operates with a reduced debt load and a refocused brand portfolio, no longer listed on the NYSE.

## Brand Portfolio

- Almay — hypoallergenic / sensitive-skin cosmetics

- Cutex — nail care
- Charlie — fragrance

## APIs

No public APIs have been documented. Revlon's partner, supplier, and retailer integrations are conducted through private channels — EDI / GDSN feeds for retail distribution, the corporate Supplier Portal for procurement, and standard Shopify storefront infrastructure for direct-to-consumer e-commerce.
